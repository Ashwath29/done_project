Let us consider a positive integer a Divide the positive integer a by 3, and let r be the reminder and b be the quotient such that a = 3b + r……………………………(1) where r = 0,1,2,3….. Case 1: Consider r = 0 Equation (1) becomes a = 3b On squaring both the side a2 = (3b)2 a2 = 9b2 a2 = 3 × 3b2 a2 = 3m Where m = 3b2 Case 2: Let r = 1 Equation (1) becomes a = 3b + 1 Squaring on both the side we get a2 = (3b + 1)2 a2 = (3b)2 + 1 + 2 × (3b) × 1 a2 = 9b2 + 6b + 1 a2 = 3(3b2 + 2b) + 1 a2 = 3m + 1 Where m = 3b2 + 2b Case 3: Let r = 2 Equation (1) becomes a = 3b + 2 Squaring on both the sides we get a2 = (3b + 2)2 a2 = 9b2 + 4 + (2 × 3b × 2) a2 = 9b2 + 12b + 3 + 1 a2 = 3(3b2 + 4b + 1) + 1 a2 = 3m + 1 where m = 3b2 + 4b + 1 ∴ square of any positive integer is of form 3m or 3m+1. Hence proved.
